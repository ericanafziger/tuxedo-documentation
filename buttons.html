Buttons

Buttons are incredibly important to any website - otherwise how would the user know where to click? Tuxedo breaks buttons down into several easy-to-use classes.

Dark Button

This is the basic dark button. It is based of whatever your dark variable is set to in your globals.scss file. Hover over the button to give it a quick test drive.

<button type="button" class="btn-dark">Button</button>

Light Button

This is the basic light button. It is based of whatever your light variable is set to in your globals.scss file. Hover over the button to give it a quick test drive.

<button type="button" class="btn-light">Button</button>

Pop Button

This is the basic pop of color button. It is based of whatever your color-pop variable is set to in your globals.scss file. Hover over the button to give it a quick test drive.

<button type="button" class="btn-pop">Button</button>

Dark Ghost Button

This is the dark version of Tuxedo’s ghost button. Ghost buttons are only the suggestion of a button with a simple outline for clean design. It is based of whatever your dark variable is set to in your globals.scss file. Hover over the button to give it a quick test drive.

<button type="button" class="btn-ghost-dark">Ghost Button</button>

Light Ghost Button

This is the light version of Tuxedo’s ghost button. It is based of whatever your light variable is set to in your globals.scss file. Since this button is a bit hard to see on a white background we threw a color behind it to make it visible. Hover over the button to give it a quick test drive.

<button type="button" class="btn-ghost-light">Ghost Button</button>

Pop Ghost Button

This is the pop of color version of Tuxedo’s ghost button. It is based of whatever your color-pop variable is set to in your globals.scss file. Hover over the button to give it a quick test drive.

<button type="button" class="btn-ghost-pop">Ghost Button</button>
